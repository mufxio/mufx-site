CFTC Net USD Positioning — 14-Year Bearish Extreme
XAU/USD — Absorbing Dollar Strength, Structural Bid Intact

DXY is testing the upper bound of its eight-month range, climbing to 97.8 on Tuesday as the new Section 122 tariffs took effect at midnight and Trump signaled a further increase to 15%. EUR/USD slipped below 1.18 for the second session, consolidating after last week's post-SCOTUS whipsaw. Gold holds above 5,150 near three-week highs — the bid is structural, not speculative, and the metal is absorbing dollar strength without breaking lower. USD/JPY pushed toward 156 after reports that PM Takaichi pressed BOJ Governor Ueda on the pace of rate hikes, temporarily removing the April hike bid that had supported the yen.

The 97.9–98.4 zone — the 200-day SMA cluster — is the level that defines whether this is a dead-cat bounce or something more consequential. CFTC positioning remains at 14-year bearish extremes on USD, which means a squeeze is mechanically possible but requires a genuine data catalyst. Friday's tariff ruling was the structural confirmation. The Section 122 replacement is weaker, time-limited, and legally constrained. This week's price action is noise from repositioning and thin post-ruling liquidity, not a regime shift.

Q2 Thesis Status: Unchanged
The Q2 thesis — structurally bearish USD, long gold, long EUR on dips — has not changed. The SCOTUS ruling was the structural confirmation, not the challenge.
Key Watch
Friday core PCE — the last major inflation datapoint before the March FOMC. A reading above 3.0% extends the hawkish pause narrative and gives the dollar correction room to run. Below 2.8% reopens the cut conversation and accelerates the trend.
