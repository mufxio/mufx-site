The Drive Function

D(Ht) = Σ|h*i − hi,t|m / n

Distance from homeostatic equilibrium in multidimensional "homeostatic space." Setpoint h* is the target P&L rate. Deviation drives behavior.

h* = homeostatic setpoint (target P&L rate)
ht = current state (actual P&L rate)
m,n = shape of drive surface (exponents)

Primary Reward

r(t) = D(Ht) − D(Ht+1)

Reward = reduction in drive. You get rewarded for moving closer to your setpoints. Hull's drive reduction theory — formalized as an RL reward signal.

"Maximizing discounted rewards is mathematically equivalent to minimizing discounted deviations from homeostatic setpoints." — Keramati & Gutkin

L2 · Safety

Empowerment

E = max I(A; S' | S)

Mutual information between actions and future states. Maximizing empowerment = keeping options open. Converges to unstable equilibria — the states where control matters most.

→ blakcat: empowerment maximization IS risk management. Stay liquid, stay agentic.
👁️
L2 · Safety Alt.

Uncertainty Reduction

r(e,t) = C·(1 − P(e,t))

Reward novel situations. Simple but flawed — gets stuck in stochastic "noisy TV" environments. Better variant: information gain (entropy reduction).

→ blakcat: not ideal alone — markets have irreducible noise. Pair with IGM.
🔥
L4 · Esteem

ICM · Curiosity (Pathak 2017)

r_i(t) = ||φ(s_t+1) − φ̂(s_t+1)||²

Curiosity = error predicting consequences of own actions in learned feature space. Features trained via inverse dynamics — captures only what the agent can influence. 4000+ citations.

→ blakcat: when satiated, explore the market driven by curiosity. Build hunting models.
🌊
L4 · Flow State

Intermediate Novelty

r = C·exp(−C₂·||Er(t) − Er_σ||²)

Goldilocks zone: reward situations neither too easy nor too hard to predict. This IS the flow state — where competence meets challenge. The sweet spot of optimal learning.

→ blakcat: seek market regimes where prediction is improving, not random noise.
🌌
L5 · Self-Actualization

Learning Progress (Schmidhuber)

r = <Er(t−θ)> − <Er(t)>

Reward = improvement in prediction/compression ability over time. The derivative of competence. "Artificial scientists" with intrinsic desire to understand. Compression progress = fun.

→ blakcat: research mode vs. hunting mode — triggered by compression progress signal.

TLO: Thresholded Lex Ordering

Lex-optimize:
[survival_hard,
safety_τ,
coord_τ,
explore_τ,
performance_max]

Each objective has a threshold τ. Once reached, further improvement provides no benefit — resources shift to the next objective. The organism doesn't need to be maximally safe — just safe enough.

Key insight: This prevents excessive conservatism. The organism isn't risk-averse by nature — it's risk-averse when necessary, and growth-oriented when safe.

Psi Theory contrast: Dynamic Competition

blakcat/EAI vs. the literature

What nobody
else does.

After exhaustive literature review, here's what's genuinely novel about the blakcat architecture — versus the existing traditions it builds on.

Novel #1

One primitive drive — everything emerges

Most systems model multiple drives from the start. blakcat starts with ONE (hunger/survival) and lets higher-order drives emerge from stability at the lower level. Yang (2023) designs the hierarchy in. blakcat wants it to emerge from the dynamics.

Novel #2

The organism metaphor applied to trading

Homeostatic RL applied to robotics and neuroscience — yes. Applied to a financial trading agent where the "body" is a portfolio and "food" is profitable trades — not found in the literature.

Novel #3

"Earning fear" as developmental milestone

The idea that an agent must earn its risk aversion through experience (not hard-coded) is consistent with fear conditioning literature — but framing it as a developmental stage in a trading organism is genuinely new.

Novel #4

Q-table initialization as genome

The Baldwin Effect applied to Q-learning: evolution operates on initial Q-values (genome), learning on runtime values. Over generations, learned fear becomes innate — shifting knowledge from runtime computation to initialization. Pre-training as evolutionary Baldwin Effect.
