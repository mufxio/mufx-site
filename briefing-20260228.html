Escalation Timeline — 28 February 2026

~02:30 EST
Trump announces "major combat operations" against Iran
Video posted to Truth Social. Operation dubbed "Epic Fury" by DoD. Stated objective: destroy missile infrastructure, prevent nuclear capability, regime change.
~03:00 EST
Israeli strikes confirmed — "Roaring Lion"
IAF reports 500 military targets struck using ~200 fighter jets. Targets include Khamenei's residence compound, presidential palace, National Security Council. Defense Minister Katz calls it a "preemptive attack." State of emergency declared in Israel.
~04:00 EST
US strikes confirmed — CENTCOM statement
Targets: Iranian missile sites, air defense systems, IRGC command and control centers. US military planning for "several days of attacks" per CNN sources. Trump tells IRGC to "lay down your arms or face certain death."
~06:00 EST
Iran retaliates — missiles fired across the Gulf
Ballistic missiles launched at Israel and US bases in Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, UAE, Jordan, Syria, and Saudi Arabia. IRGC declares all US and Israeli assets in the region "legitimate targets." Fifth Fleet HQ in Bahrain struck directly. One civilian killed in Abu Dhabi. Jordan intercepted 49 drones and ballistic missiles.
~08:00 EST
Strait of Hormuz closure reported
UK Maritime Trade Operations agency reports sea vessels received closure notices. Strait handles ~20 million barrels/day (~20% of global consumption). Gulf states close airspace. Houthis announce resumption of Red Sea attacks.
~10:00 EST
Khamenei status unknown
Israeli officials claim Khamenei was "hit." Iranian FM Araghchi tells NBC that Khamenei and the president are alive "as far as I know." Satellite imagery shows Khamenei's Tehran residence destroyed. Communications cut across much of Iran.
SATURDAY
Weekend market proxies react
Gold perps on Hyperliquid surge +5% to ~$5,464. Silver perps +8% to ~$97.50. Oil perps +6.2%. Bitcoin drops 3.8% to ~$63,000 as risk-off dominates. $128B erased from crypto market cap. Traditional FX, equity, and commodity markets remain closed until Sunday evening (Asia) / Monday morning.

The strikes came hours after Oman's foreign minister reported a diplomatic "breakthrough" — Iran had reportedly agreed to zero enrichment stockpiling and full IAEA verification. The timing is significant. Whether this constitutes a failure of diplomacy or a deliberate exploitation of diplomatic cover for a military operation will be debated extensively.

Iranian Red Crescent reports over 200 killed and 700 injured in the initial wave. Iranian state media reports a strike hit a girls' primary school in Minab, southern Iran, killing at least 53 students. Saturday is the first day of the Iranian work and school week. The US military reports no American casualties as of early afternoon. OPEC+ meets Sunday; the cartel may consider a larger production increase above the planned 137,000 bpd if supply disruption materializes.

This is not a limited strike. Trump explicitly stated the objective is regime change. Netanyahu said the joint operation will last "as long as needed." US military sources told CNN the Pentagon is planning several days of attacks. Iran's IRGC has declared operations will "continue relentlessly until the enemy is decisively defeated." This language on both sides points to an extended campaign, not a one-day event.

Thesis Impact
The Q2 bearish-USD / long-gold thesis just absorbed a regime-change-level geopolitical shock. In the short term, the dollar may catch a safe-haven bid (history shows DXY typically rallies in the first days of a military conflict). This does not invalidate the structural thesis — it introduces a temporary counter-current. Gold benefits under both scenarios: safe-haven demand in escalation, inflation premium in de-escalation if oil stays elevated. The key variable is duration. A multi-week campaign changes the macro landscape entirely. A 48-hour strike normalizes within days. Watch the Strait of Hormuz — if it stays closed through Monday, oil above $80 reprices everything.
